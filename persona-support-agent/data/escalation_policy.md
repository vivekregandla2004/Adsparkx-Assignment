# Escalation Policy

## Overview

This document describes when customer support issues are escalated, how the escalation process works, and what customers can expect during escalated cases.

---

## Escalation Triggers

### Automatic Escalation

The following conditions trigger automatic escalation to a senior support agent or specialist team:

**1. Unresolved After Multiple Interactions**
- If the same issue is raised 3 or more times without resolution within a 7-day window
- The system automatically flags the ticket and assigns it to a Tier 2 specialist

**2. Billing and Financial Disputes**
- Any billing dispute exceeding $200
- Duplicate charge complaints
- Unauthorized transaction reports
- Refund requests on annual plans

**3. Security Incidents**
- Suspected account compromise
- Unauthorized access reports
- Data breach concerns
- Credential theft

**4. Legal and Compliance Issues**
- GDPR data deletion requests ("Right to be Forgotten")
- CCPA opt-out and data access requests
- Legal holds or court orders
- Terms of service violation disputes

**5. Service Outage Impact**
- Customer reports business-critical downtime caused by our service
- Data loss reported
- SLA breach claims

**6. Account Deletion Requests**
- Permanent account deletion requests must be reviewed by a human agent
- Data portability requests

### Manual Escalation by Agent

Support agents may escalate at their discretion when:
- The issue requires access to systems or data beyond their authorization level
- The customer has expressed extreme dissatisfaction
- Technical complexity exceeds Tier 1 expertise
- The issue affects multiple customers (potential systemic problem)

## Escalation Levels

### Tier 1 — Front-Line Support
- **Scope:** Standard inquiries, password resets, basic troubleshooting, plan information
- **Response time:** Within 4 business hours (Professional), immediate chat (Enterprise)
- **Tools:** Knowledge base, account dashboard access, basic account modifications

### Tier 2 — Specialist Support
- **Scope:** Complex technical issues, billing disputes <$500, account recovery
- **Response time:** Within 2 business hours
- **Tools:** Full account history, API log access, billing system, escalation to engineering

### Tier 3 — Engineering / Subject Matter Experts
- **Scope:** Bugs, data issues, system-level problems, enterprise custom configurations
- **Response time:** Within 1 business hour (critical), 4 hours (standard)
- **Tools:** Full system access, database access, deployment capabilities

### Tier 4 — Leadership / Legal
- **Scope:** Legal matters, major financial disputes, executive-level customer concerns
- **Response time:** Case-by-case
- **Tools:** Full authority to resolve and offer non-standard remedies

## Escalation Process

### Step 1: Identification
The support agent identifies that a ticket meets escalation criteria.

### Step 2: Documentation
Before escalating, the agent documents:
- Customer's issue summary
- Steps already attempted
- Customer's emotional state / satisfaction level
- Urgency level

### Step 3: Handoff
The agent transfers the ticket to the appropriate tier with a structured handoff summary including:
- Customer persona and communication style
- Issue history and timeline
- Relevant documents retrieved from the knowledge base
- Recommended next steps

### Step 4: Customer Notification
The customer receives an email notification:
- Confirmation that their case has been escalated
- Ticket reference number
- Expected response time from the next agent

### Step 5: Resolution and Follow-Up
After resolution, a follow-up survey is sent within 24 hours to confirm the issue is resolved and measure satisfaction.

## Customer Expectations During Escalation

| Action | Timing |
|--------|--------|
| Escalation acknowledgment email | Within 30 minutes |
| First contact from escalated team | Per tier SLA above |
| Status update (if not resolved) | Every 4 hours during business hours |
| Resolution notification | Upon resolution |
| Follow-up satisfaction survey | Within 24 hours of resolution |

## Escalation Metrics

We track escalation performance to improve support quality:
- Escalation rate (target: <15% of all tickets)
- Time to resolution post-escalation (target: <24 hours for Tier 2)
- Customer satisfaction post-escalation (target: >4.0/5.0)
- First-contact resolution rate (target: >70% for Tier 1)
